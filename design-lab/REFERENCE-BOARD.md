# Premium Visual Ceiling — Reference Board for Cricket Underworld

**Compiled:** 2026-07-09 · **Researcher:** Sanjaya (research only — no verdicts) · **Purpose:** re-brief the design after the first comp scored 1/10. Every link is click-and-see, accessed 2026-07-09.

> **Why this exists:** the first design comp reused the game's *existing* tokens, which capped the ceiling at "current game, tidied up." The founder's expectations are "at a completely different level." This board calibrates against the real premium ceiling so the next comp starts from art direction, not token rearrangement.

---

## The one-line answer

Top card/sports/gacha apps don't win on *better panels* — they win on **art, ceremony, and material**: a rendered human/hero occupies the card, the card looks like a physical foil/metal object, and pulling/buying/bidding is staged as a multi-second theatrical reveal with lighting, sound, and a number that visibly *climbs*. Cricket Underworld's flat dark-glass chrome has none of these — **it renders data, not desire.**

---

## The 5 premium levers Cricket Underworld is missing (priority order)

1. **A human on the card.** Every money-tier reference (FUT, eFootball, Dream11) puts a rendered player/hero illustration or portrait as the emotional center. Our cards are text + numbers on glass — no face, no body, no world. *Single biggest gap.*
2. **Card-as-object (foil / metal / holo / animated background).** FUT, Marvel Snap, Hearthstone make the card look and move like a physical premium collectible — foil splits, metallic tiers, per-card golden animation, backgrounds that spill past the card edge. Our cards read as UI rectangles, not treasure.
3. **The reveal ceremony.** FUT's tunnel "walkout," Genshin's wish-to-gold streak, Clash Royale's chest crack — 3–8 second staged suspense → payoff events. We drop cards instantly; the dopamine spike is left on the table.
4. **Money staged as an event, with numbers that climb.** Monopoly GO / Coin Master and the FUT walkout make currency a sensory spectacle (climbing counters, coin-rain, cheer SFX, screen juice). Our bids/prices are static number swaps — money on screen, but not money that *feels expensive*.
5. **A color and lighting world.** Gold-for-rarity color theory, dramatic rim lighting, depth/parallax, stadium atmosphere. We use one dark-blue glass palette flat across every screen — no light source, no depth, no place.

### Special focus — the "live money moment" (bid / buy / big number climbing)
- **Climbing counters + coin-rain + cheer SFX + screen juice** — Monopoly GO / Coin Master deconstructions: https://www.blog.udonis.co/mobile-marketing/mobile-games/monopoly-go · https://playliner.com/2308-monopolygo (deliberate "dopamine rush").
